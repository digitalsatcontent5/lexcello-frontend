Sample Question Preview

Get a taste of the question quality and format you'll encounter in Practice Test 1:

Passage: The government's decision to increase university tuition fees will inevitably lead to a decrease in higher education participation among working-class students. This is because working-class families, unlike their middle-class counterparts, cannot afford to pay higher fees or take on additional debt. Therefore, the policy effectively creates a barrier that prevents social mobility through education.
Question: Which of the following best identifies an assumption made in this argument?
  1. A) Middle-class families always have sufficient funds for university fees
  2. B) Working-class students are less academically capable than middle-class students
  3. C) Higher education is the primary means of achieving social mobility
  4. D) The government intends to reduce working-class participation in higher education
Explanation: The correct answer is C. The argument assumes that higher education is the primary means of achieving social mobility. Without this assumption, the conclusion that the policy "prevents social mobility through education" would not follow logically from the premises.
