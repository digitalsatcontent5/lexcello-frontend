Frequently Asked Questions About Our LNAT Practice Tests

Everything you need to know about preparing with Lexcello

How many LNAT practice tests should I complete?

We recommend completing all 10 LNAT practice tests in our series. Most successful students complete at least 6-8 full-length practice tests before their actual LNAT exam. Our progressive series ensures you build skills systematically from foundation to advanced level.

Are these practice tests similar to the real LNAT exam?

Yes! Our LNAT practice tests are designed to exactly match the real LNAT format introduced in 2024. Each test contains 42 multiple-choice questions, uses the 95-minute time limit, and replicates the digital interface you'll see at Pearson VUE test centers. The difficulty level and question types mirror authentic LNAT passages and questions.

Can I retake the practice tests multiple times?

Absolutely! You can retake any LNAT practice test as many times as you want. Each attempt is saved in your performance analytics dashboard, allowing you to track improvement over time. Many students retake tests to improve their scores and build confidence.

Do I need to download any software?

No downloads required! Our LNAT practice tests run entirely in your web browser. Simply create a free account, and you can start practicing immediately on any computer or tablet with internet access.

How is Test 1 different from Tests 2-10?

Test 1 is completely free and provides full access to 42 LNAT practice questions with the same features as premium tests (timing, explanations, analytics). Tests 2-10 offer progressive difficulty levels and cover a wider range of reasoning patterns and passage types. The premium tests help you systematically build skills for the actual LNAT exam.

What universities accept the LNAT?

The LNAT is required for undergraduate law programs at Oxford, Cambridge, LSE, UCL, Durham, King's College London, University of Bristol, University of Glasgow, SOAS, and Jindal Global Law School (India). Our practice tests are designed to help you excel in admissions to all these institutions.
